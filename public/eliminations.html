Elimination Prep Guide

10 Rules To Survive (and Win) Elimination

The notes below turn the raw eliminations.txt brief into a fast, readable playbook. Every rule is built from years of event lessons: relentless activity beats stats, unity beats ego, and timing turns good teams into terrifying ones.

01

Activity beats raw stats

Tickets move with successful attacks, not with muscle alone. A lineup of 2M grinders online all day can outpace 50M idle monsters.

  • Keep people online around the clock.
  • Chain attacks relentlessly.
  • Push coordinated bursts near every elimination tick.

Presence is power. Even modest stats become deadly at 200+ hits per day.

02

Dogpile the weakest team

During the elimination phase, the lowest-ticket team loses a life each tick. Almost always, the smartest move is to hit the bottom team together.

  • It keeps your tickets out of last place.
  • It guarantees the weakest team dies faster.
  • It avoids chaotic multi-directional losses.

Routine: check the board, hit the lowest, repeat.

03

Protect tickets as hard as you steal them

Every hit against you burns a ticket. Defense and revival discipline stop leaks before they erase your offense.

  • Keep inactives revived; clear hospitals often.
  • Use self-hosp or travel when someone becomes a drain.
  • Help weaker teammates dodge attack windows.

A player who dies 100 times can undo three heavy hitters.

04

Attack in waves near ticks

Timed waves turn average rosters into efficient war machines. Stack hits where they matter most.

  • Organize tight 30-minute waves.
  • Swing hardest just before the elimination tick.
  • Time revives so outgoing hits peak during the window.

Think drum machines: relentless rhythm swings hundreds of tickets.

05

Coordinate targets, not random hits

Unfocused attacks waste energy on impossible fights. Define who gets hit and in what order.

  • Set target teams and priority players.
  • Maintain sortable hit lists by online status.
  • Give fallback options for lower-stat attackers.
06

Shield your ticket banks

Players holding piles of tickets become glowing targets. Keep them safe or lose momentum fast.

  • Identify who is carrying tickets.
  • Hide them in hospital or travel when heat rises.
  • Assign revive support and avoid exposing them near ticks.

Predators always hunt the fattest antelope.

07

Use travel as defense

Travel removes a player from the battlefield. It is the timeout bench for ticket liabilities or focused targets.

  • Pull liabilities off the board before they bleed tickets.
  • Break enemy surges by taking pressure targets out.
  • Let chased players reset and return safely.
08

Stay organized

Coordination is worth millions of stats. Clarity keeps a team deadly day after day.

  • Use Discord, in-game chat, and shared spreadsheets.
  • Run shift rotations so coverage never lapses.
  • Keep target lists current as the board shifts.
09

Kill drama fast

Teams usually collapse from ego, not skill. Rogue hitters burn tickets and drag everyone down.

  • Stop off-plan attacks immediately.
  • Park personal grudges until the event ends.
  • Reward players who follow the plan every cycle.
10

Play for the team

The winning mindset is collective. Support keeps morale high through 10 to 12 exhausting days.

  • Revive others and share intel.
  • Trade food and boosters freely.
  • Encourage lower-stat players and keep spirits up.

This event is a marathon with knives. Good vibes carry the finish.

Treat this as a living doc. Adjust targets as the scoreboard shifts, keep revival lines open, and remember: disciplined waves beat raw power every single year.
